The correct reading: train_sem under single-seed single-run is not a predictor of val_mIoU. The "aux choking sem" story was a plausible narrative attached to a non-significant signal. Retract it.

  **4. Peak at ep45, not ep68+.** CR-P top-10 val points are all ep36-ep62, peak ep45. Post-ep45 val oscillates 0.66-0.72 with no second peak through ep78. CR-L by contrast peaked at ep68 and oscillated 0.69-0.72 from ep32 onwards (longer plateau before peak). CR-P's earlier + flatter peak means running CR-P to ep100 will not produce a better number — ep100 will sit in the 0.66-0.72 band.

  **Implications (what this means for v2.0):**
  - **Aux-head route ceiling at ~0.725 is now confirmed across two architectures** (single-stream CR-L, dual-stream CR-P). Both are −1pp below CR-A and statistically indistinguishable from each other. The gap is closed by changing the experimental axis away from "more/better aux head", not by more tuning within that axis.
  - **CR-M is now low value.** Same architecture as CR-P, same boundary_target source (voxel-threshold via `edge[:,3]>0.5`), different loss (CR-L's BoundaryBinaryLoss vs CR-P's PureBFANetLoss). Given CR-L ≈ CR-P, CR-M is very likely to land in the same 0.72-0.73 band. Still valuable to run if queue is free (isolates loss form), but no longer likely to change the verdict.
  - **CR-N now matters for a different reason.** Not for "does the aux route work" (answered: no), but for "does BFANet's naive BCE collapse on single-stream at 7% positive" — the cross-arch control for finding 1. CR-P showed it does not collapse in dual-stream; CR-N tells us whether that was the architecture's fault or the loss's property.
  - **CR-O is now the single most important remaining experiment.** It is the only route in the matrix without any aux head at all (soft-weighted CE on semantic logits only). If CR-O beats CR-A, boundary information still has value via CE weighting even though the aux head ceiling is at ~0.725. If CR-O matches or loses to CR-A, the verdict for v2.0 is "no form of boundary-aware supervision on this dataset under the current voxel geometry beats pure semantic CE".
  - **train_sem vs val_mIoU decoupling retract.** Any v2.0 conclusion that relied on train_sem-based diagnosis (CR-L "zombie aux", "aux choking sem from ep1") needs to be downgraded to "not a reliable signal under single-seed noise". The val_mIoU curves are the ground truth; train loss is too sensitive to loss term weighting to compare across experiments.

  **Decision:** Do NOT wait for CR-P to reach ep100. Close it at ep78 with verdict recorded. Queue CR-N and CR-O next.

- [v2.0] 100 epochs for clean-reset experiments (user preference over 150)
- [v2.0] Route redesign: reinterpret support as boundary proximity cue, not geometric regression
- [v2.0] BCE on continuous target has irreducible entropy lower bound — replaced with MSE+Dice (CR-H)
- [v2.0] Semantic CE boundary upweight uses continuous support with truncation at >0.5 (CR-I)
- [v2.0] Module g redesigned from semantic→boundary (failed) to boundary→semantic gating (g v3 in CR-J, then g v4 cross-stream fusion in CR-M)
- [v2.0] CR-L binary threshold = 0.5 (d < 2.35cm ≈ voxel radius), positive ratio ~2% after voxel (3.32% raw, verified from 10-scene sample)
- [v2.0] CR-L pos_weight = 1: sample_weight_scale=9 already handles class imbalance; combined 45x was too heavy
- [v2.0] Grid voxel size (6cm) caps achievable positive ratio — threshold cannot be raised beyond 0.5 without data pre-processing changes
- [v2.0] **CR-H failed on full train** — continuous support target (even with MSE+Dice) does not recover the CR-B gap. Further continuous-target work under Part 1 is deprioritized; the active path is the BFANet-style binary threshold (CR-L/M).
- [v2.0] **CR-J dropped before training** — g v3 (per-channel gate) superseded by g v4 (cross-stream fusion attention) in CR-M. No value in running CR-J with the older gating module.
- [v2.0] **BFANet radius is r=0.06 m absolute** (not "3× voxel"). On ScanNet `0.06 = 3 × 0.02` is a coincidence of the mink-path voxel size; octformer uses `r=0.006` because coords are pre-scaled by /10.25. Do NOT port the 3× voxel identity to other datasets — use the absolute 6 cm. Evidence: `train.py:82-87` in weiguangzhao/BFANet@master.
- [v2.0] **CR-M deliberately kept on `edge[:,3]>0.5`** — not migrated to `boundary_mask_r060` so the CR-L vs CR-M comparison isolates architecture (single-stream vs dual-stream g v4), not boundary-target definition. CR-N and CR-P are the experiments that get the new precomputed mask.
- [v2.0] **CR-P v1:v2 = 1:1** (corrected 2026-04-11 afternoon alignment pass, commit 3452189) — BFANet's actual code sums `sem_v1 + dice_v1 + margin_v1 + margin_dice_v1 + sem_v2 + dice_v2 + margin_v2 + margin_dice_v2` with no per-branch scaling. The earlier `0.5:1.0` "v2 is primary, v1 is regularizer" framing was a Claude mechanism interpretation without BFANet code basis and has been retracted. CR-P and CR-M now share 1:1 weighting; they differ only in loss (PureBFANetLoss vs BoundaryBinaryLoss) and boundary target (r=0.06 mask vs `edge[:,3]>0.5`).
- [v2.0] **Aux-head route ceiling at ~0.725 is confirmed across architectures** (2026-04-11 evening, from CR-P ep1-78 post-hoc analysis). CR-L (single-stream, best 0.7251 @ ep68) and CR-P (dual-stream + g v4 + faithful PureBFANetLoss, best 0.7241 @ ep45) are Δ=0.001 apart, both ~1pp below CR-A 0.7336, both statistically indistinguishable from each other under single-seed noise (~0.05-0.07). The gap to CR-A is closed by *changing the experimental axis away from "more/better aux head"*, not by further tuning within that axis. CR-M is now predicted to land in the same band and is a low-value experiment unless queue is free.
- [v2.0] **train_loss_semantic is NOT a predictor of val_mIoU under single-seed single-run conditions** (2026-04-11 evening, retraction). The 2026-04-10 "CR-L aux crowds sem gradient from ep1" diagnosis was falsified by CR-P: CR-P's train_sem is 0.18-0.64 HIGHER than CR-L at every matched epoch while val_mIoU is identical (Δ=0.001). Any v2.0 conclusion relying on train_sem-based diagnosis (CR-L "zombie aux", "aux choking sem from ep1") is downgraded to "not a reliable signal". val_mIoU curves are the ground truth; train loss is too sensitive to loss-term weighting to compare across experiments.
- [v2.0] **BFANet naive BCE (pos_weight=1) does NOT collapse at 7% positive in dual-stream (CR-P)** — Dice climbed 0.14→0.72 over 78 epochs with clean prob_pos/neg separation. The CR-N collapse-risk hypothesis is disproven in dual-stream; CR-N single-stream still has to prove it independently. This does not move the val_mIoU verdict — a well-trained boundary head inside CR-P still produced val_mIoU ≈ CR-L. "Boundary head learns well" ≠ "semantic val improves".
- [v2.0] **g v4 fusion collapses to near-identity in CR-P** (v2 − v1 ≈ 0.003-0.007 on all metrics through ep78). The K=48 patch attention + zero-init residual was designed to fix BFANet's degenerate ES Attention K=1, but in practice the fusion path still has no meaningful amplitude. CR-P's "dual-stream architectural gain" is effectively zero. Any future fusion-module redesign needs a mechanism that produces non-trivial v2 − v1 delta during training, not just at init.
